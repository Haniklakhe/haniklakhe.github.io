Biography

About Hanik

I'm a Water Engineering and Management graduate student at the Asian Institute of Technology (AIT), Thailand (GPA: 3.87/4.0), with a deep focus on hydrology, climate change, and sustainable water resource management.

My research integrates hydrological modelling, remote sensing, GIS, and AI/ML to solve real-world water challenges — from flood risk evaluation in Northern Thailand watersheds to suspended sediment dynamics in Himalayan rivers.

As Project Leader of the MESSHI Project (2023–2024), I established a suspended sediment laboratory from scratch, deployed real-time OBS sensors in the Himalayas, and managed a citizen science team — with findings published in EGUsphere (2026) and presented at AGU 2023 and WaterSciCon24.

Previously at Smartphones For Water Nepal for 5 years, I contributed to peer-reviewed publications, managed water monitoring datasets, and represented the organization at international conferences including multiple AGU Fall Meetings.

I'm open to research collaborations, academic positions, and water sector opportunities worldwide.
